Getting There

We recommend using public transport to travel to and from Alnwick. The closest train station is Alnmouth. There are regular, direct trains from London Kings Cross that take approximately 3 and a half hours (quite impressive when you consider it is around 500km). The best time to buy tickets is roughly 8-12 weeks from when you would like to travel. We use trainline.

From Alnmouth it is a short taxi (10 minutes) into Alnwick. There is also a bus that runs every half an hour that takes 20 minutes.

We suggest hiring a car if you would like to explore more of Northumberland. This is probably best done from Newcastle-upon-Tyne as this is the closest major city.

Where to Stay

Below is a list of recommendations for reasonably priced places to stay in Alnwick. The weddinng will be during UK school holidays so we suggest securing accommodation soon.

The White Swan The hotel is located at the centre of town and is set in a 300-year old coaching inn.
The Oaks Hotel This hotel is also centrally located in Alnwick in a smaller building with 13 rooms.
Premier Inn This budget hotel is located 5 minutes drive from the main town.
The Hogs Head Inn Also located slightly outside the main town, this is another good option for those on more of a budget.
Other options There are a selection of boutique cottages and holiday homes available on Booking.com and Airbnb. However, just to let you know, we had quite a few challenges securing our own accommodation through these platforms because many of the options required a minimum amount of nights or to check in and out on specific days of the week that did not align with our dates.
